Biography

Virginie Vandenbulcke graduated as Business Engineer from the Catholic University of Louvain (FUCaM Campus, Mons, Belgium) in 2011.
In 2016, as part of her doctoral studies, she completed a research stay at the Texas State University (TX, USA) and collaborated with Professor Ekstrand in the field of collaborative recommendation systems.
She graduated with a master's degree in information and communication in 2017.
In 2019, she presented her thesis, Personalized Collaborative Recommendations in the Mass Retail Sector, in the joint fields of marketing science and data mining at the Catholic University of Louvain.
In October 2019, she was hired as a project manager at CLICK (FEDER digistorm project) in the Num√©diart Institute (UMONS, Belgium).
Today, she holds a postdoctoral position as a senior researcher in the ISIA LAB (laboratory of UMONS), focusing on:
- Motion capture and analysis of the resulting data;
- Intelligent spaces (smarts spaces);
- Understanding and navigating multimedia data;
- Speech and sound technologies;
- Virtual reality and augmented reality.
